# Template: Cohort / Community Introduction

**Use when:** Outreach to university clubs, community organizations, DAOs, or cohort-style groups where you're introducing RaidGuild or a member's services/mentorship.

## Merge Fields
- `{{orgName}}` — Organization name
- `{{orgSummary}}` — Brief description of what they do (from directory)
- `{{senderName}}` — Your name
- `{{senderRole}}` — Your role / affiliation
- `{{connectionPoint}}` — Why this org is relevant (shared interest, local connection, etc.)
- `{{offer}}` — What you're offering (workshop, mentorship, guest talk, collaboration, etc.)
- `{{callToAction}}` — Specific next step

---

## Email

**Subject:** {{connectionPoint}} — intro from {{senderName}}

Hi {{orgName}} team,

I'm {{senderName}}, {{senderRole}}. I found your org through the university directory and {{connectionPoint}}.

I work with RaidGuild, a collective of web3 builders (developers, designers, project managers) working on Ethereum and decentralized tech. We're always looking to connect with groups like yours.

{{offer}}

If that sounds interesting: {{callToAction}}

Best,
{{senderName}}

---

## Variant: Recruiting / Talent Pipeline

**Subject:** Web3 careers — workshop for {{orgName}}?

Hi {{orgName}} team,

I'm {{senderName}} from RaidGuild, a decentralized builder collective in the Ethereum ecosystem. We noticed your org focuses on {{orgSummary}}.

We're looking to connect with students and early-career folks interested in web3 development, design, and project management. Would your org be interested in a guest talk or workshop on careers in decentralized tech?

Happy to tailor it to your members' interests. Just reply and we can figure out timing.

Cheers,
{{senderName}}

---

## Notes
- These orgs are NOT clients — they're community/recruiting pipeline
- Tone should be collaborative, not salesy
- Mention specific things about the org to show you actually looked at their page
- University orgs often have high leadership turnover — emails may bounce at semester boundaries
